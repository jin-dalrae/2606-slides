## The Question

### Can Cosmos rebuild the community wall for VR?

Offline communities already use spatial, asynchronous surfaces: poster walls, bulletin boards, sticky-note walls, and message walls.

People read by walking, scanning, getting closer, noticing density, and returning to a remembered spot.

Cosmos asks whether VR can rebuild that public wall pattern: persistent, spatial, low-pressure, and readable without requiring everyone to be live together.

<p class="slide-closing">This is not a new social VR room. It is an offline spatial community pattern rebuilt as an async VR place.</p>

## One-Slide Answer

### Cosmos should start as a VR community wall

The research points to one clear direction:

Cosmos should rebuild offline spatial async community before becoming a full social platform.

Do not start by recreating the cocktail party. Start by rebuilding the wall.

Its first job is to help users leave, browse, compare, and return to community messages by place instead of by feed rank.

## What I Found

<p class="slide-ref">Research summary</p>

1. Reading without posting is normal and often strategic.
2. Offline communities already use spatial async walls.
3. Feeds are good at volume, weak at place memory.
4. Spatial audio proves location helps attention, but live voice is hard to scale cleanly.
5. Social VR is spatial, but often too synchronous and presence-heavy.
6. XR reading comfort is fragile.
7. AI summaries are useful but need source trails.
8. Cosmos should prove the wall before becoming a platform.

## What Do Users Want?

The first research question is not:

> How do we get people to post in Cosmos?

It is:

## Reading Without Posting Is Real

<p class="slide-ref">Literature</p>

Online community research found that people read silently for many reasons: they get what they need, learn the group first, avoid adding noise, struggle with software, or dislike group dynamics.

> "most lurkers are not selfish free-riders"

<p class="slide-source">Source: Preece, Nonnecke, Andrews<br><a href="https://www.socs.uoguelph.ca/~nonnecke/research/CHB_Proof.pdf">socs.uoguelph.ca</a></p>

---

## Why People Stay Quiet

<p class="slide-ref">Reading behavior</p>

| Reason | Cosmos implication |
| --- | --- |
| Just reading is enough | Reading must be a success state |
| Still learning the group | Show tone and norms |
| Others already said it | Reduce pressure to add noise |
| Shy or uncertain | Support low-pressure browsing |
| Poor fit or dynamics | Reveal conflict before entry |

---

## The Majority May Not Post

<p class="slide-ref">Participation inequality</p>

Nielsen Norman Group describes participation inequality as a common community pattern:

> "90% of users are lurkers"

That number is not a universal law, but it is a useful design warning.

<p class="slide-closing">A product for discussion should not ignore the reader majority.</p>

---

## Design For Readers First

<p class="slide-ref">Section takeaway</p>

The first success metric should not be how many people post.

It should be whether users can understand:

- What the major positions are
- Where disagreement is strongest
- Which comments support a label
- Whether they want to return later

---

## Feed Fatigue Is Part Of The Problem

<p class="slide-ref">Social media fatigue</p>

Research on social media fatigue connects overload, stress, comparison, privacy concerns, and compulsive use with fatigue.

> Information overload was the strongest predictor of social media fatigue.

<p class="slide-source">Source: Frontiers in Psychology, 2024<br><a href="https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2024.1277846/full">frontiersin.org</a></p>

---

## The Feed Solves Discovery, Not Structure

| Feed pattern | User cost | Cosmos test |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Endless vertical scroll | No clear stopping point | Spatial regions |
| Engagement ranking | Outrage can dominate | User-directed path |
| Single ordered stream | Debate structure collapses | Stance clusters |
| Context switching | Threads fragment | Whole-discussion map |

---

## Algorithmic Help Has A Cost

<p class="slide-ref">Algorithmic dependence</p>

Algorithmic apps can create gratification, but research also links algorithmic dependence with fatigue.

Cosmos should not become another system that owns the user's path.

<p class="slide-closing">The agentic layer should guide, not drive.</p>

## But Reddit Is Still A Feed

Reddit is excellent for content supply.

- Which stance is where?
- Which claim repeats?
- Which group is underrepresented?

## Discord Has Community Continuity

<p class="slide-ref">Competitive gap</p>

Discord is strong for persistent community identity.

But context fragments across:

- Channels
- Threads
- Voice
- Forum posts
- Time

<p class="slide-closing">Cosmos can map discussion across fragments.</p>

---

## Social VR Has Space

<p class="slide-ref">Competitive gap</p>

VRChat and social VR prove that spatial social behavior matters.

But they are mostly synchronous, voice-forward, and presence-heavy.

Cosmos should borrow spatial memory without requiring live performance.

---

## Dolby Proves Spatial Voice Matters

<p class="slide-ref">New evidence</p>

Paul Boustead's Dolby IO talk frames spatial audio as a solution to the cocktail party problem: real rooms have overlapping speech, laughter, and affirmations, but people use spatial release from masking to focus.

This supports Cosmos's larger bet: when conversation gets dense, location can reduce cognitive load.

<p class="slide-source">Source: Paul Boustead, Dolby IO, "Spatial Communications at Scale in Virtual Environments"</p>

---

## But Live Audio Is Not The Wedge

<p class="slide-ref">Strategic implication</p>

Dolby's scale work also shows the trap:

- N-loudest selection cuts quiet speakers
- N-closest can create one-way hearing bugs
- Fixed hearing ranges pop voices in and out
- Large attenuated ranges are best, but expensive
- Dense rooms need server-side mixing, noise suppression, and exact voice leveling

<p class="slide-closing">Cosmos should spatialize existing async discussion first, then treat live presence as an optional later layer.</p>

---

## Spatial Operating Systems Have Windows

<p class="slide-ref">Competitive gap</p>

Vision Pro and Quest can put browser windows in space.

That does not automatically make discussion spatial.

Cosmos should not place a Reddit feed on a sphere or wall and call it done.

<p class="slide-closing">The structure of the conversation has to become spatial.</p>

---

## AI Summaries Are A Competitor

<p class="slide-ref">Competitive gap</p>

AI summaries are fast.

But they can flatten disagreement, overstate certainty, and hide source context.

Cosmos should compete by preserving structure and evidence, not just compressing text.

---

## Structure Has Been Tried Before

<p class="slide-ref">Closest prior art</p>

Argument-mapping tools like Kialo and Debategraph have spatialized debate for two decades.

The research is cautionary:

- Maps grow visually complex
- Building them is real work
- Adoption stays thin, even in 2D

Cosmos inverts the model: read first, no manual mapping, AI clusters wall content for users to inspect.

<p class="slide-source">Source: Argument-visualization and public-deliberation tool studies<br><a href="https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3461564.3461584">dl.acm.org</a></p>

## The Missing Middle Is A Discussion Map

<p class="slide-ref">Section takeaway</p>

Cosmos is strongest between:

- Flat feeds that hide structure
- Live rooms that demand presence
- Spatial voice systems that require heavy real-time infrastructure
- AI summaries that hide source trails

<p class="slide-closing">The market gap is spatial async sensemaking.</p>

## What Should Cosmos Aim For?

<p class="slide-ref">Research question 3</p>

The strategic question:

Should Cosmos become a full social platform, or first rebuild the offline community wall as a VR place?

---

## Platform First Is Too Risky

Platform-first means solving four hard problems at once:

1. Empty content
2. New community norms
3. Moderation and safety
4. New posting behavior

That is too much before the spatial value is proven.

---

## Wall First Is Testable

Wall-first means rebuilding a familiar offline pattern with controlled content:

- User-provided note-wall and bulletin-wall references
- Permission-cleared Discord exports
- Class and community discussion boards
- Prompted sticky-note wall sessions
- Research and product-feedback datasets
- Synthetic test datasets

The prototype can test spatial value without building a full social platform.

<p class="slide-closing">The research needs a meaningful wall, not a specific platform.</p>

---

## Strategy Path

| Phase | Cosmos behavior | Evidence needed |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Rebuild a VR wall with controlled notes | Users scan and orient better than in a feed |
| 2 | Add saved paths and regions | Users return to remembered places |
| 3 | Add lightweight note leaving | Users want async contribution |
| 4 | Add native community or live presence | Users need Cosmos-native social behavior |

---

## Content Access Still Matters

<p class="slide-ref">Strategic honesty</p>

Wall-first reduces platform cold start, but still needs content.

Reddit now gates all API access, bars AI use of its data without a license, and is litigating scrapers.

| Stage | What it needs | Rights exposure |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Research now | Permission-cleared wall, class, or discussion dataset | Low: participant-created or synthetic data |
| Product later | Source platforms or public communities at scale | High: licensing, ToS, platform dependence |

<p class="slide-closing">Rights are a build-stage gate, not a research-stage blocker.</p>

<p class="slide-source">Source: Reddit API/AI policy 2025; Reddit v. Anthropic; Reddit v. Perplexity<br><a href="https://caldwelllaw.com/news/reddit-perplexity-ai-lawsuit-contract-data-rights/">caldwelllaw.com</a></p>

## What Interaction Points Are Worth Testing?

<p class="slide-ref">Research question 4</p>

The research does not say "build a spatial surface for its own sake."

It says the VR wall must survive specific risks:

- Reading comfort
- Cognitive load
- AI trust
- Device fit
- Source inspection
- Place memory

---

## Risk 1: Spatial Layout Becomes Decorative

Spatial design is only useful if it reveals relationships.

Structured-debate tools stumbled here: structure that looked rich but cost more attention than it returned.

Cosmos must help users answer:

- What are the major camps?
- Where is disagreement strongest?
- Which claims repeat?
- Which voices are missing?

---

## Spatial Layout Test

| Good sign | Bad sign |
| --- | --- |
| Users remember where ideas were | Users hunt for text |
| Regions clarify stances | Regions feel arbitrary |
| Labels help orientation | Labels replace judgment |
| Users choose paths | Users feel lost |

---

## Risk 2: Headset Reading Is Fragile

<p class="slide-ref">XR comfort</p>

VR comfort research keeps cybersickness and individual susceptibility in focus.

Text-heavy interfaces also need careful readability design.

Cosmos must measure comfort directly, not assume the headset experience works.

---

## Reading Rules

<p class="slide-ref">Design requirements</p>

- Stable cards
- High contrast text
- Short line length
- Predictable focus state
- No forced head movement for basic reading
- Zoom and flat view fallback
- Desktop baseline

---

## Risk 3: Devices Want Different Depths

<p class="slide-ref">Market signal</p>

IDC reports XR growth led by smart glasses, while traditional VR/MR headsets remain more specialized.

Cosmos should not be headset-only.

<p class="slide-source">Source: IDC XR market update<br><a href="https://www.idc.com/promo/arvr/">idc.com</a></p>

---

## Device Roles

| Device | Cosmos role |
| --- | --- |
| Desktop | Baseline reading, importing, survey scale |
| Quest / MR | Immersive map and spatial memory |
| Vision Pro-style device | High-resolution spatial reading |
| Smart glasses | Saved highlights and return prompts |

---

## Risk 4: AI Labels Lose Trust

<p class="slide-ref">AI trust</p>

AI summaries can be convincing while wrong.

Microsoft Research describes the risk of:

> "convincing yet incorrect outputs"

Implication: Cosmos cannot make labels feel like final truth.

---

## No Label Without A Trail

Every cluster, label, tension marker, and missing-voice prompt should answer:

- Which posts caused this?
- What evidence supports it?
- How confident is the system?
- Can the user disagree?
- Can the user hide it?

---

## Risk 5: Live Presence Takes Over

<p class="slide-ref">Dolby IO implication</p>

Spatial voice is valuable, but the hard part is not just rendering.

It is deciding who each listener hears, at what distance, with what attenuation, suppression, leveling, and latency.

Cosmos should not let live audio become the first proof of value.

<p class="slide-closing">First prove the map. Add live presence only if users need it.</p>

---

## Comfort And Trust Are Product Requirements

<p class="slide-ref">Section takeaway</p>

Cosmos is not validated by looking interesting.

It is validated only if users can:

- Read comfortably
- Navigate confidently
- Understand more
- Inspect sources
- Keep agency
- Avoid live-room pressure unless they choose it

---

## What Evidence Do We Need Next?

<p class="slide-ref">Research question 5</p>

The next study should not ask:

> Do you like Cosmos?

It should ask:

> Can you understand the same conversation better here than in a flat feed?

---

## Core Research Claim

### A VR wall should improve sensemaking

Given the same set of notes, users should be able to identify:

- 2-3 major stances
- 1-2 tensions
- A missing or underrepresented voice
- The source posts behind a label
- Where important regions were

More comfortably than in a flat feed.

---

## Prototype Test 1

### Flat feed vs VR wall

| Condition | User sees |
| --- | --- |
| A | Same posts in normal vertical feed |
| B | Same posts on a Cosmos VR wall |

Task: explain the major positions and point back to where they were.

---

## Prototype Test 2

### Label trust

Show AI-generated region labels.

Ask:

- What does this label mean?
- Which posts support it?
- Does it feel helpful or pushy?
- Would you rename it?

---

## Prototype Test 3

### Missing voices

Show a missing-voice prompt.

Ask:

- What do you think is missing?
- Does this feel useful or presumptuous?
- Would you want Cosmos to show this?
- How should uncertainty be worded?

---

## Prototype Test 4

### Saved path

Let users browse, save a path, and return.

Measure:

- Do they remember the regions?
- Do labels help recall?
- Would they reopen the path later?
- Would they share the path?

---

## Prototype Test 5

### Device mode fit

Test which tasks belong where:

| Task | Likely device |
| --- | --- |
| Long reading | Desktop / Vision Pro-style |
| Spatial overview | Quest / MR |
| Return prompt | Smart glasses |
| Import and editing | Desktop |

## Survey

The survey should measure behavior, not general interest:

- How often people read without posting
- Why they stay quiet
- Whether feeds feel exhausting
- Whether voice feels demanding

---

## Success Metrics

| Goal | Metric |
| --- | --- |
| Sensemaking | User identifies stances and tensions |
| Place memory | User remembers where notes or regions were |
| Low pressure | User says browsing does not require replying |
| Trust | User traces labels to source posts |
| Comfort | User reports low strain and can continue |
| Return value | User saves or wants to revisit a path |
| Strategy | User prefers wall-first or explains why not |

---

## Stop Criteria

Cosmos should be questioned if:

- Users understand the flat feed faster
- The spatial wall feels decorative
- The wall metaphor does not help orientation
- Headset reading is too tiring
- AI labels reduce trust
- Users do not want saved paths
- Experts see unsolved moderation risk

---

## Go Criteria

Cosmos becomes more credible if:

- Users identify stances faster in Cosmos
- Users describe it as calmer or more orienting
- Users remember locations like an offline wall
- Source inspection increases trust
- Users want to return to saved paths
- Experts agree wall-first reduces risk
- Device roles become clear
