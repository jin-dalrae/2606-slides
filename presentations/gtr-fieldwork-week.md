## Founders stopped at concept and trust before they judged layout

<p class="slide-ref">Overview</p>

Five paper-prototype sessions tested whether startup founders would complete intake and read assessment concepts. When product purpose, value proposition, or document sensitivity failed, onboarding A/B and report layout did not matter.

## We asked for layout preferences before earning trust

<p class="slide-ref">Testing Process · mistakes</p>

<div class="cards-2">
  <article>
    <h3>Upload before benefit</h3>
    <p>Step 2 asked for pitch deck and accounts before participants knew what they would receive.</p>
    <p>> "What am I getting out of this? I would probably stop right here." — Ted</p>
  </article>
  <article>
    <h3>Jargon without definitions</h3>
    <p>Net impact, maturity 1–5, projection, and milestones required interviewer explanation.</p>
    <p>> "What does Net Impact mean? Do people know what levels 1, 2, 3, and 5 mean?" — Caroline</p>
  </article>
  <article>
    <h3>SaaS labels did not read</h3>
    <p>Footprint/handprint cards assumed hardware examples. Caroline inferred handprint from remote-work transport avoidance for Deltic.</p>
  </article>
  <article>
    <h3>Too many concepts per session</h3>
    <p>Onboarding A/B, report A/B, dashboard, storyboard, and sharing in one sitting — long sessions, diluted signal.</p>
  </article>
</div>

<p class="slide-closing">Held single-session red flags; changed when the same issue repeated across participants.</p>

## Trust and clarity failed before any metric made sense

<p class="slide-ref">Stakeholders · learn</p>

| Signal | Who said it | So what |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Upload pitch deck = IP risk without upside | Caroline, Ted, Brian | Default away from document upload; manual spend fields first |
| Product purpose unclear before intake | Amali, Ted, Brian | State audience and deliverable above the fold |
| Visual report beats text wall | Caroline, Josh, Brian; Amali wants A+B | Lead artifact is infographic + explanatory text |
| Dashboard too dense horizontally | Josh, Brian, Amali | Vertical stack; goals and milestones on top |
| SaaS cost = cloud/AI tokens, not hardware | Ted, Caroline | Business-model-specific intake defaults |

## Four ideas held up across sessions

<p class="slide-ref">Stakeholders · validated</p>

<div class="cards-2">
  <article>
    <h3>Four-step intake works</h3>
    <p>Profile → optional documents → EMITS/AVOIDS → generate — when jargon is explained inline.</p>
  </article>
  <article>
    <h3>Presets beat typing</h3>
    <p>Amali, Caroline, Josh, and Brian preferred dropdowns and checkboxes over free text.</p>
  </article>
  <article>
    <h3>+ / − framing resonates</h3>
    <p>Separate footprint and handprint ledgers make sense when labels match business model.</p>
  </article>
  <article>
    <h3>Concept rated positively</h3>
    <p>Amali: clean, understandable, idea is good. Caroline: interesting and cool.</p>
  </article>
</div>

## Feedback moved five product decisions

<p class="slide-ref">Changes · pivots</p>

| From | To | Who drove it |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Default file upload | Manual spend fields; optional incubator path | Ted, Brian, Caroline |
| Text-primary report | Visual + explanatory text (combine A+B) | Amali, Caroline, Josh, Brian |
| Horizontal dashboard cards | Vertical stack; goals/milestones on top | Josh, Brian |
| Generic EMITS checklist | Business-model defaults (SaaS tokens, etc.) | Ted, Caroline |
| Public LinkedIn share | Positive metrics only; default private | Josh |

## Six gaps fieldwork week did not close

<p class="slide-ref">Changes · challenges</p>

<div class="cards-3">
  <article>
    <h3>Onboarding A vs B</h3>
    <p>Amali and Caroline preferred step-by-step; Josh preferred scroll with step count. No winner at n=5.</p>
  </article>
  <article>
    <h3>Document path untested</h3>
    <p>No real files, incubator integration, or trusted third-party context in sessions.</p>
  </article>
  <article>
    <h3>Numbers are illustrative</h3>
    <p>Footprint differed between report print and dashboard sketch — not validated emissions data.</p>
  </article>
  <article>
    <h3>Copy still needs product definitions</h3>
    <p>Net impact, maturity, projection, milestones — interviewer explanation is not scalable.</p>
  </article>
  <article>
    <h3>Peer benchmark scope</h3>
    <p>Amali requested leaderboard and peer comparison — undefined for MVP privacy and data model.</p>
  </article>
  <article>
    <h3>Larger-company segment</h3>
    <p>Caroline and Brian suggested bigger companies — not tested this round.</p>
  </article>
</div>
